    • Analysis of a toxigenomics RNA-Seq data set

    In September 2014,the Sequencing Quality Control (SEQC)consortium published an entire series of studies addressing the question of how well RNA-seq performs, compared to microarrays and qRT-PCR, in the journal Nature Biotechnology. As a basis for the Steered Research Project we selected the work by Wang et al. (Nat Biotech. 2014, 32, 926-932). This study used RNA seq to examine the effect of 27 drugs/chemicals, covering seven modes of action, on gene expression in rat liver. The effect of drugs on gene expression (and alternative splicing) is an important question when it comes to evaluating the wanted and unwanted effects of drugs and drug candidates. The data of this study are publically available from the NCBI short read archive (SRP039021). This allows us to reproduce some of the analysis (at least we hope so) and add further value to the work of the SEQC consortium.
